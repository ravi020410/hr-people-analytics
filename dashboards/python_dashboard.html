The Economics of Attrition

DTC brands and technology organizations often overlook employee attrition as a primary cost driver. Standard industry models prove that the voluntary resignation of a skilled professional costs approximately **1.5 times their annual salary** in recruitment fees, interviewing overhead, onboarding friction, and lost productivity during ramp-up.

This corporate HR analytics showcase connects synthetic employee demographics, salary databases, and exit survey logs to establish **strategic retention visibility**. By highlighting compensation deficits (Compa-Ratio deviations) and departmental voluntary exit motives, HR directors can shift from reactive replacement to proactive compensation mapping.

Immediate Retention Interventions

  • Engineering Compensation Realignment: Software Developers with performance scores of 4 or 5 are currently operating under an average Compa-Ratio of **86.4%**. Deploy an immediate $35,000 localized salary adjustment pool to realign these 15 key players with fair market rates before voluntary resignations trigger recruitment fees.
